<!-- # About -->

Ruomeng Liu ([pronunciation](https://translate.google.com/?sl=zh-CN&tl=zh-CN&text=%E5%88%98%E8%8B%A5%E8%92%99&op=translate): /ʐwɔ.məŋ ljoʊ/) is an M.Phil. candidate in Communication at [the University of Hong Kong (HKU)](https://www.hku.hk/), supervised by Prof. [King-wa Fu](https://sites.google.com/site/fukingwa/). Before that, he completed a Bachelors's degree in Journalism at [University of Chinese Academy of Social Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Chinese_Academy_of_Social_Sciences) in 2024, advised by Prof. [Fen Xiang](http://mediaresearch.cn/bsxz/mkszyyjs/202405/t20240517_5752230.shtml).

Broadly I investigate how people make decisions in high-choice media environments (and its political consequences). I am interested in computational social sciences and cognitive science.

<!-- # Research

My research focuses on political communication and media cognition in digital media contexts. I use quantitative methods to study how attention, knowledge, decisions, and communication are affected by high-choice media environments, and the political consequences of these *processes*.

Methodologically, I am interested in designing and improving computational methods, with a particular focus on measurement problems and model/analytical considerations in causal inference. I am drawn to questions about the [commensurability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commensurability_(philosophy_of_science)) of different research designs and what each can and cannot tell us.
